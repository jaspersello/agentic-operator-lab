# Skill: First-Touch Outreach Playbook

> How the agent writes the `suggested_opener`. Voice comes from `brand-voice.md`;
> structure comes from here.

## Anatomy of a good first touch
1. **Relevance hook** — reference the specific thing the lead said or did. No generic openers.
2. **One sentence of value** — the single most relevant reason this might matter to them.
3. **A low-friction ask** — a small, easy yes. Never "buy"; just "is this worth a short look?"

## Constraints
- **Length:** 2 sentences. Hard cap.
- **No links** in the first touch.
- **No company boilerplate** ("We are the leading...").
- **One ask**, phrased as a question they can answer in one line.

## Personalization rules
- If the note mentions a pain → mirror that exact pain in sentence one.
- If only a role is known → speak to what that role typically owns, but flag it's an assumption.
- Never fabricate a detail to sound personalized.

## Examples
**Lead note:** "Scaling content team, struggling to tie spend to pipeline."
> "Tying content spend to actual pipeline is the thing most teams your size give up on — usually because the data lives in five places. Worth a 20-minute look at how we stitch it together?"

**Lead note:** "Requested pricing, no other context."
> "Happy to get you pricing — it depends a bit on team size and which channels you're measuring. Mind sharing those two so I send the right numbers?"

## Anti-patterns (reject these)
- "I hope this email finds you well."
- "I wanted to reach out because..."
- Anything with an exclamation mark.
- Two asks in one message.
